Abulafia is a reimagining of the software in Umberto Eco’s novel Foucault’s Pendulum (1988). In the novel the protagonists use what was cutting-edge technology to blur the lines between fact and fiction and generate conspiracies through the software’s ability to create connections from their inputs. This updated version of Abulafia presents a more gamified and ‘casinofied’ experience. The player picks from a deck of cards that all present a separate conspiracy. Each card contains a prompt, and once ten are picked they are combined into a “grand conspiracy”, generated using the Grok API and then printed on a thermal printer. Abulafia reflects the precipice where truth and reality stand amid disinformation and AI driven distortion. Truth and reality are being gambled with by tech companies who bow to none. 

Abulafia is a desktop only experience that is best played in your browsers full screen mode.
